#+title: 2 Pointers

* All patterns with logic
** 1. Collision Pattern (Opposite Ends)
Move inward from both ends based on a sorted property or a bottleneck to reduce the search space.
** 2. Partitioning Pattern (Read/Write)
Maintain a "processed" vs. "unprocessed" boundary using one pointer to explore and another to track the last valid position.
** 3. Search Space Reduction (k-Sum)
Reducing complex multi-variable problems (like 3Sum or 4Sum) into simpler, nested Two Sum problems.
** 4. Counting Pattern (Boundary Logic)
Calculating ranges or number of pairs in $O(1)$ instead of $O(n)$ by leveraging the distance between pointers.
** 5. Two Array Traversal (Merge Pattern)
Coordinated movement across two separate data structures, typically processed in a single pass.
** 6. String Logic (Skip & Compare)
Handling "noise" (non-alphanumeric characters, backspaces) while comparing elements from both ends.
** 7. Fast & Slow Pointers (Tortoise and Hare)
Moving one pointer faster than the other to find cycles or specific midpoints in structures without indices.
** 8. Final Boss (Prefix/Suffix & Boundary Invariants)
Managing multiple boundaries or global state simultaneously to solve problems with complex spatial constraints.

* How to practice
After every problem, write:
- Invariant
- Pointer movement rule
- Why movement is safe

Example for 167. Two Sum II:
Invariant:
- Array is sorted.

Movement rule:
- If sum too small → move left.
- If sum too large → move right.

Why safe:
- Sorted order guarantees discarded pairs
- cannot become valid later.
